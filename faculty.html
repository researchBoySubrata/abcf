I am an assistant professor in the Department of CSE at IIT Madras. I received my PhD from SUNY Stony Brook, NY (advisor: Samir R. Das) and had a three-year stint as a researcher at NEC Labs America, Princeton, NJ. Earlier, I received a B.E. in computer science from Jadavpur University.

I am broadly interested in designing IoT-based/mobile systems that interact with and interpret the physical world, spanning both algorithm design as well as system prototyping. My current research focuses on intelligent sensing using everyday devices such as smartphones or autonomous mobile platforms like robots or drones. I employ such sensing to develop solutions for indoor localization, smarthomes or intelligent human computer interaction interfaces. I am also interested in exploring how such systems can potentially benefit from state-of-the-art machine learning tools/algorithms.

I am looking for sincere students in the area of networked systems, specifically, along the lines of the following topics:
(i) Machine learning assisted sensing in IoT systems,
(ii) Indoor localization technologies.
